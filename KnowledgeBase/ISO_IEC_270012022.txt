ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — KEY SECURITY CONTROLS REFERENCE

A.5 ORGANIZATIONAL CONTROLS

A.5.15 Access Control
Access to information and associated assets shall be restricted in accordance with the
established access control policy. Users shall only be provided with the access to
networks and network services that they have been specifically authorised to use.

A.5.16 Identity Management
The full lifecycle of identities shall be managed. This includes creation, modification,
and deletion of identities, ensuring that each identity is uniquely assigned and that
shared accounts are prohibited for privileged access.

A.5.17 Authentication Information
Allocation of authentication information shall be controlled by a management process,
including advising personnel on appropriate handling of authentication information.
Multi-factor authentication shall be used for all remote access and privileged operations.

A.8 TECHNOLOGICAL CONTROLS

A.8.2 Privileged Access Rights
The allocation and use of privileged access rights shall be restricted and managed.
Privileged accounts must be reviewed at least quarterly. Any unexplained or unauthorised
use of privileged rights is a security incident requiring immediate investigation.

A.8.5 Secure Authentication
Secure authentication technologies and procedures shall be implemented based on information
access restrictions and the access control policy. Brute-force protection must be active
on all authentication endpoints.

A.8.7 Protection Against Malware
Protection against malware shall be implemented and supported by appropriate user awareness.
Disabling security controls (including firewalls and IDS) without authorisation is
equivalent to a malware-facilitated attack and must be treated as a HIGH severity incident.

A.8.15 Logging
Logs that record activities, exceptions, faults and other relevant events shall be produced,
stored, protected and analysed. Key events to log: logins (success and failure), privileged
operations, access to sensitive data, configuration changes, and security control changes.

A.8.16 Monitoring Activities
Networks, systems and applications shall be monitored for anomalous behaviour and
appropriate actions taken to evaluate potential information security incidents. Monitoring
must include after-hours activity, unusual data volumes, and geographic anomalies.

A.8.20 Network Security
Networks and network devices shall be secured, managed and controlled to protect
information in systems and applications. Changes to network security controls require
formal authorisation and must be logged with the approving authority.

INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION UNDER ISO 27001

Category 1 — Data Breach Indicators:
- Bulk data export without authorisation
- Unusual outbound data transfers
- Access to sensitive data stores outside business hours

Category 2 — Account Compromise Indicators:
- Multiple failed authentication attempts (brute force)
- Successful login immediately following repeated failures
- Login from a previously unseen IP address
- Privilege escalation outside approved workflow

Category 3 — Insider Threat Indicators:
- Audit log deletion or modification
- Security control disablement (firewall, IDS, AV)
- Account creation or privilege grant outside normal hours
- Access to data beyond the user's normal role

Category 4 — System Integrity Indicators:
- Unauthorised configuration changes
- New scheduled tasks or services created
- SSH key additions to privileged accounts
- Unexpected outbound network connections
