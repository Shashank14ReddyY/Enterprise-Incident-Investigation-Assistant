ENTERPRISE INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Version 3.2 | Effective Date: January 2024

1. ACCESS CONTROL POLICY

1.1 Authentication Requirements
All users must authenticate using multi-factor authentication (MFA) before accessing
any enterprise systems. Single-factor authentication is prohibited for privileged accounts.

Account lockout policy: Accounts must be automatically locked after 3 consecutive failed
login attempts within a 10-minute window. Locked accounts require administrator review
before re-activation.

1.2 Privileged Access Management
Privileged accounts (admin, root, service accounts) must follow the principle of least
privilege. Privilege escalation must be logged, reviewed, and approved through the change
management process. Service accounts must not be used for interactive logins.

1.3 Account Lifecycle
New accounts must be created only through the approved provisioning workflow. Account
creation by privileged users outside the provisioning system is a policy violation and
must be investigated as a potential security incident. All account changes must be logged.

2. NETWORK SECURITY POLICY

2.1 Firewall Management
Firewall configurations must not be modified without an approved change request. Disabling
firewall rules, especially during off-hours, must trigger an automatic security alert.
All-traffic-allow rules (0.0.0.0/0) are prohibited in production environments.

2.2 Data Exfiltration Prevention
Bulk data exports exceeding 10,000 rows must be pre-approved by a data steward.
Transfers to external IP addresses must be logged and reviewed within 24 hours.
VPN tunnels to non-approved external endpoints are prohibited.

3. INCIDENT RESPONSE POLICY

3.1 Severity Classification
- CRITICAL: Active data breach, ransomware, complete system compromise
- HIGH: Privilege escalation, credential theft, firewall disabled in production
- MEDIUM: Multiple failed logins, unauthorised access attempts, policy violations
- LOW: Single failed login, minor configuration drift

3.2 Response Times
- CRITICAL: Immediate response, notify CISO within 15 minutes
- HIGH: Response within 1 hour, notify security team
- MEDIUM: Response within 4 hours
- LOW: Reviewed in next business day security review

3.3 Evidence Preservation
Audit logs must be preserved and must not be altered or deleted during an investigation.
Deletion of audit logs is itself a HIGH severity incident.

4. AUDIT AND LOGGING POLICY

4.1 Log Retention
All system logs must be retained for a minimum of 12 months. Security-relevant logs
(authentication, privilege changes, firewall events) must be retained for 24 months.

4.2 Log Integrity
Logs must be written to a write-once, tamper-evident log store. Log clearing or
modification by any account other than the designated log management service account
is a critical security violation.

4.3 Monitoring
Continuous monitoring must cover: failed authentication attempts, privilege escalation,
firewall changes, bulk data access, off-hours administrative activity, and SSH key changes.
